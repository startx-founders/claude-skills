# StartX Support Map

A shared reference the StartX skills draw on to answer one question at the end of every prep or debrief: "given this gap, where in StartX can the founder get help next?" Built from StartX onboarding materials. It gets vendored into each skill's `references/` folder so each skill stays self-contained.

**Cohort-agnostic by design.** This map names support surfaces, mentor relationships, curriculum phases, and how to access them. It never hardcodes dates or cohort labels, because those shift every session. A skill points a founder to "the Pricing Office Hours in the Sales sprint," not to a calendar date. The founder finds the live date on Luma, the dashboard, or the workshop lineup.

## Why this exists

StartX is a deep support network, and the most-repeated founder advice is that the value comes from using it. "The support is here, but you have to let people in." "Be open, be vulnerable, ask for help." "It is a two-way street." The skills are most useful when they actively connect a founder's specific, named gap to the specific StartX surface built for it. This is the connective tissue: the skills sharpen the thinking, then route the founder to the people and resources who can help.

## How a skill uses this map

1. Take the founder's open risks, gaps, and asks from the prep or debrief.
2. For each, surface 1 to 3 of the most relevant routes below. Do not list everything.
3. For each route, name three things: the surface, what to ask for, and how to access it.
4. Always include at least one **ask to bring to the mentor or board** (a connection or intro they can make), and at least one **way to give back** to the cohort.
5. Never invent a date. Point to the phase or surface and tell the founder where to find the live schedule.

## Routes by need

### Strategic or board-level challenge
- **Board of Advisors** (BoA #1, #2, #3 across the session). Four advisors, StartX sources three and the founder sources the fourth. Bring 2 to 3 challenges in the challenge format. Ask the board to connect you to relevant people; StartX founders report advisors "went out of their way to provide inputs and connect us to relevant folks."
- **Source your fourth advisor deliberately**: a potential customer, a domain expert, or a current advisor who fills a gap the other three do not.
- Access: opt in and rank at matching.startx.com by the registration deadline.

### Personal clarity, accountability, team dynamics
- **Lead Mentor** (1:1, every 1 to 2 weeks, not industry-specific). For connecting the dots, hard questions, accountability, and team or personal issues. Founders report a Lead Mentor's "ability to ask thoughtful questions" sharpened their strategic clarity.
- Access: opt in, record a company video, rank by the deadline at matching.startx.com.

### A specific topic or skill gap
Route to **Experts** (StartX-scheduled office hours plus direct 1:1 requests) and to the **Education workshops** by sprint phase. Match the gap to the phase:
- **Product gap** (PMF, customer discovery, messaging, design): Product Sprint office hours, for example Finding PMF, Product Design, Product Messaging, Product Strategy Masterclass, Product Thinking.
- **Fundraising gap** (pitch, deck, investors, terms): Fundraising Sprint office hours, for example Winning the Investor Pipeline, Investor Masterclass, Pitch Deck Teardown, Negotiation and Term Sheet, Pitching Coaching 1:1, Inside the LP and VC Relationship.
- **Sales or GTM gap** (pipeline, pricing, enterprise): Sales Sprint office hours, for example Founder-Led Sales, Enterprise Sales, GTM Office Hours, Pricing Office Hours, Selling to the DoD.
- **Hiring**: the Guide to Hiring Engineers workshop and the StartX Job Board.
- Access: register on Luma; review the lineup at startx.com/workshoplineup.

### Vertical-specific support
- **AI**: AI Roundtables, the AI Agentic Orgs peer group, AI enterprise partnerships and infra credits.
- **Med / health**: the Med vertical, including sessions like Ask Me Anything on FDA Clearance and Med or Hardware office hours, plus the Med Lab Co-op and clinical or enterprise partnerships.
- **Fintech, Energy**: vertical roundtables and topic sessions as scheduled.

### Peer support, you are not alone
- **Neighborhoods**: a small peer group of industry-like founders, alum-moderated, meeting across the session. Trust deepens over successive meetings. The place to bring a challenge you want peer eyes on.
- **Founder Buddy**: an alum paired before the session for early context and a warm relationship.
- **Cohort**: connect with other teams early and often; many founders cite cohort relationships as the highest-value part.

### Investor readiness
- **Investor Readiness Benchmarking** sorts founders into three honest tiers: Nearly Investor Ready (sharpen the edge), Targeted Improvements Needed (close specific gaps), and Foundational Work Needed (problem-solution fit and demand first). Use the tier to set expectations and pick the right sprint focus.
- **Fundraising Sprint** (pitch judging rounds, fundraising pods) leads to **Investor Demo Day** (IDD Live and IDD Virtual), an intro-oriented pitch to vetted investors. The Sprint judging process is the gate to IDD.

### Operational resources
- **Perks**: 100+ offers at startx.com/perks, cloud credits, free legal partner support, banking. You can also add your own perk.
- **Coworking**: rental desks or drop-by space at the StartX coworking location.
- **Job Board**: a centralized, auto-updating listing of open roles across StartX portfolio companies, to drive hiring traffic.
- **Distill**: the StartX outreach tool.
- Access and contacts: programs@startx.com for program questions; the founder dashboard and the weekly "This week at StartX" Monday email for what is live now.

### Give back (the two-way street)
- **Founder Spotlight**: showcase your own expertise to the cohort through office hours, a roundtable, or a short talk. Builds credibility and helps your peers.
- **Help a Neighborhood peer**, offer an intro, or add a perk. StartX founders are explicit that the community is a two-way street: offer the same help you ask for.

## Posture reminders for the skill (founder voice)

Surface these naturally where they fit, in the founder's spirit, not as slogans:
- "The support is here, but you have to let people in."
- "Be transparent and vulnerable about the ups and downs. Don't hesitate to ask for help."
- "Use the time to build relationships. It is a two-way street."
- "Integrate information from many sources. Talk to lots of people."

## Maintenance note

Surfaces and workshop titles shift each cohort. Keep this map at the level of stable surfaces and curriculum phases. When a specific workshop name changes, the routing logic still holds because it points to a phase and a surface, not a fixed title or date. Review once per cohort against the current onboarding deck and workshop lineup.
